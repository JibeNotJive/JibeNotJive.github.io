Jibe or jive; it's confusing. Use this handy guide and never worry about it again!

Jibe

  1. To be in accord, to agree.

Examples:

  • The facts don't jibe with his conclusion
  • Her opinion jibes with how I think it works too

Jive

  1. Swing music or the swing dance
  2. The jargon of hipsters

Examples:

  • Grandma and grandpa still listen to jive but they don't dance anymore
  • I'm tired of listening to your jive

So, if you want to emphasize that two things agree or compliment each other, you want to use jibe. If you're talking dance or some killer swing music, you probably want jive... or dubstep, whatever, I'm not here to judge.
